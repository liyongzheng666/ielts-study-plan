## 〇、阅读原文（Reading Passage）

**Polar Bears: What Their Genes Could Teach Us（续）**

④Physiologists Alanda Lennox and Allen Goodship found an explanation for this paradox in
2008. They discovered that pregnant bears were able to increase the density of their bones
before they started to build their dens. In addition, six months later, when they finally
emerged from the den with their cubs, there was no evidence of significant loss of bone
density. Hibernating brown bears do not have this capacity and must therefore resort to
major bone reformation in the following spring. If the mechanism of bone remodelling in
polar bears can be understood, many bedridden humans, and even astronauts, could
potentially benefit.

⑤The medical benefits of the polar bear for humanity certainly have their importance in our
conservation efforts, but these should not be the only factors taken into consideration.
We tend to want to protect animals we think are intelligent and possess emotions, such as
elephants and primates. Bears, on the other hand, seem to be perceived as stupid and in
many cases violent. And yet anecdotal evidence from the field challenges those assumptions,
suggesting for example that polar bears have good problem-solving abilities. A male bear
called GoGo in Tennoji Zoo, Osaka, has even been observed making use of a tool to manipulate
his environment. The bear used a tree branch on multiple occasions to dislodge a piece of
meat hung out of his reach. Problem-solving ability has also been witnessed in wild polar
bears, although not as obviously as with GoGo. A calculated move by a male bear involved
running and jumping onto barrels in an attempt to get to a photographer standing on a
platform four metres high.

⑥In other studies, such as one by Alison Ames in 2008, polar bears showed deliberate and
focussed manipulation. For example, Ames observed bears putting objects in piles and then
knocking them over in what appeared to be a game. The study demonstrates that bears are
capable of agile and thought-out behaviours. These examples suggest bears have greater
creativity and problem-solving abilities than previously thought.

⑦As for emotions, while the evidence is once again anecdotal, many bears have been seen to
hit out at ice and snow – seemingly out of frustration – when they have just missed out on a
kill. Moreover, polar bears can form unusual relationships with other species, including
playing with the dogs used to pull sleds in the Arctic. Remarkably, one hand-raised polar
bear called Agee has formed a close relationship with her owner Mark Dumas to the point where
they even swim together. This is even more astonishing since polar bears are known to
actively hunt humans in the wild.

⑧If climate change were to lead to their extinction, this would mean not only the loss of
potential breakthroughs in human medicine, but more importantly, the disappearance of an
intelligent, majestic animal.

## 一、精读批注：标出"同义替换"（阅读上分的命门）

雅思阅读考的是**识别换皮**。把题干会怎么改写原文，提前标出来：

```
原文 found an explanation for this paradox  → 题干可能写 solved / explained the puzzle
原文 increase the density of their bones      →  make their bones denser / stronger
原文 no evidence of significant loss          →  bones did not weaken noticeably
原文 must resort to major bone reformation    →  have to rebuild their bones / rely on rebuilding
原文 could potentially benefit                →  may be helped / stand to gain
原文 anecdotal evidence challenges those assumptions → informal observations question those beliefs
原文 making use of a tool                     →  using an implement / object
原文 deliberate and focussed manipulation      →  intentional, careful handling
原文 greater creativity than previously thought →  more inventive than once believed
原文 form unusual relationships with other species → build unexpected bonds with other animals
原文 actively hunt humans in the wild          →  prey on / attack people in nature
```

> 成果标准：每篇至少标出 **5–8 组**。上面给了 11 组，足够。重点盯 paradox→puzzle、
> resort to→rely on、previously thought→once believed 这类"换皮高发区"。

## 三、同义替换组（攒进总库，阅读+写作通用）

```
解释/解开：explain = account for = shed light on = resolve（a paradox/mystery）
出现：emerge = appear = arise = come out（反 disappear / vanish）
被迫采取：resort to = fall back on = turn to = rely on（含无奈）
认为/视为：perceive = regard = view = see ... as = consider
未经证实：anecdotal = unverified = informal = hearsay（反 empirical / scientific）
故意：deliberate = intentional = calculated = on purpose（反 accidental）
灭绝：extinction = dying out = disappearance（go extinct = die out）
比以前更…：greater than previously thought = more ... than once believed
```

## 四、句型库（每篇捞 2–3 个可复用结构）

```
① 条件 + 潜在收益（描述"若能弄懂，则有望受益"）
   If the mechanism of X can be understood, many ... could potentially benefit.
   → 套用：If the causes of burnout can be understood, many employees could potentially benefit.

② 让步转折"然而传闻证据挑战了既有假设"（先抑后扬）
   Bears seem to be perceived as stupid. And yet anecdotal evidence challenges those assumptions.
   → 套用：Online courses are seen as low-quality. And yet recent data challenges that assumption.

③ "这就更令人惊讶了，因为……"（用反差强化）
   This is even more astonishing since polar bears are known to actively hunt humans.
   → 套用：This is even more impressive since the team worked with almost no funding.
```

## 六、练习题 + 答案 + 归因（做完必须归因）

**True / False / Not Given**
```
Q1 Pregnant polar bears strengthen their bones before they enter their dens.
   答案：TRUE
   依据：¶4 "pregnant bears were able to increase the density of their bones before they
        started to build their dens" → 正面对应。
Q2 Brown bears lose no bone density during hibernation.
   答案：FALSE
   依据：¶4 "Hibernating brown bears do not have this capacity and must therefore resort to
        major bone reformation" → 棕熊没有这能力，需春天大修，与题干相反。
Q3 GoGo learned to use the tree branch by copying other bears in the zoo.
   答案：NOT GIVEN
   依据：¶5 只说 GoGo 用树枝够肉，从未提"模仿其他熊学会的" → 找不到 = NG。
```

**Matching Headings（给段落选标题）**
```
¶4 → "Cracking the bone-density paradox"（主旨 = 2008 研究：孕熊提前增密、禁食后不流失）
¶5–6 → "Smarter than we give them credit for"（主旨 = 工具使用、游戏行为，问题解决能力）
¶7 → "Signs of an emotional life"（主旨 = 挫败情绪、跨物种关系，如 Agee）
心法：抓段落主旨句，别被 GoGo、barrels、Mark Dumas 这些细节例子带偏。
```

**Summary Completion（NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS）**
```
A 2008 study showed pregnant bears could increase the _______ of their bones. → density
Brown bears lack this ability and must _______ major bone reformation later.  → resort to
GoGo used a tree branch as a _______ to reach meat hung out of his reach.      → tool
注意：数词数！density / tool 为 1 词，resort to 为 2 词，均合规；都直接取自原文，别自己换词。
```

**错题归因示范（这步最值钱）**
```
假设我把 Q3 选成 FALSE：
错因 = 把"原文没提（NG）"误判成"原文否定（F）"。文章只说 GoGo 会用树枝，从没交代它"怎么学会的"，
       是我脑补了"不是模仿来的"。
固化规则：T/F 必须能在原文定位到对应正句或反句；定位不到、只是"没说" = NG。
       不要把"原文未提及"当成"原文否定"。
```

## 七、口语 Part 2 + Part 3 范例（band 7.5）+ 批注

**Part 2 题卡**：*Describe a wild animal you think is intelligent.*

```
I'd like to talk about the polar bear, which I always assumed was just a big, slow predator
until I read about how clever it actually is. What changed my mind was a few real examples
from zoos and the wild.

For instance, there's a male bear called GoGo in a zoo in Osaka who figured out how to use a
tree branch as a tool — he'd swing it to knock down a piece of meat that had been hung out of
his reach. To me that's genuine problem-solving, not just instinct. In another study, bears
were seen stacking objects into piles and then knocking them over, almost as if they were
playing a game, which suggests they're curious and creative.

What surprised me even more is the emotional side. Some bears apparently hit out at the ice in
frustration when they miss a kill, and there's even a hand-raised bear that swims with its
owner. So for me the polar bear is a perfect example of an animal we underestimate — it's far
more intelligent and sensitive than its reputation suggests.
```
```
批注：开头点题并制造"反转"(assumed... until)；把文章学到的事实(GoGo 用树枝 / 堆物游戏 / Agee)
用口语复述出来，不是背书；figured out / almost as if / apparently 等地道口语标记；具体例子 +
个人评价("to me that's genuine problem-solving") = 内容具体 + 流利。
```

**Part 3 延伸**：*Do you think humans tend to underestimate animals' intelligence?*

```
Yes, I'd say we definitely do, and usually for quite shallow reasons. We tend to value animals
that look intelligent or seem emotional — like elephants or primates — and we write off others,
such as bears, as stupid or just aggressive. But a lot of that is based on anecdotal impressions
rather than real evidence. Once researchers actually observe these animals closely, they often
find deliberate, thought-out behaviour we'd never expected. That said, I think the bigger risk
isn't just underestimating them intellectually — it's that we then care less about protecting
them, and losing a species could mean losing scientific breakthroughs we can't even predict yet.
```
```
批注：先给立场(yes) 再分"为什么误判 + 后果"两层；直接复用阅读核心词 anecdotal / deliberate /
breakthroughs；用 "that said" 做让步、"write off" 显语言成熟度；不极端、有平衡 = 7.5 的样子。
```
