## 〇、阅读原文（Reading Passage）

**Polar Bears: What Their Genes Could Teach Us**

①Polar bears are being increasingly threatened by the effects of climate change, but
their disappearance could have far-reaching consequences. They are uniquely adapted to
the extreme conditions of the Arctic Circle, where temperatures can reach −40°C. One
reason for this is that they have up to 11 centimetres of fat underneath their skin.
Humans with comparative levels of adipose tissue would be considered obese and would be
likely to suffer from diabetes and heart disease. Yet the polar bear experiences no such
consequences.

②A 2014 study by Shi Ping Liu and colleagues sheds light on this mystery. They compared
the genetic structure of polar bears with that of their closest relatives from a warmer
climate, the brown bears. This allowed them to determine the genes that have allowed
polar bears to survive in one of the toughest environments on Earth. Liu and his
colleagues found the polar bears had a gene known as APoB, which reduces levels of
low-density lipoproteins (LDLs) – a form of 'bad' cholesterol. In humans, mutations of
this gene are associated with increased risk of heart disease. Polar bears may therefore
be an important study model to understand heart disease in humans.

③The genome of the polar bear may also provide the solution for another condition, one
that particularly affects our older generation: osteoporosis. This is a disease where
bones show reduced density, usually caused by insufficient exercise, reduced calcium
intake or food starvation. Bone tissue is constantly being remodelled, meaning that bone
is added or removed, depending on nutrient availability and the stress that the bone is
under. Female polar bears, however, undergo extreme conditions during every pregnancy.
Once autumn comes around, these females will dig maternity dens in the snow and will
remain there throughout the winter, both before and after the birth of their cubs. This
process results in about six months of fasting, where the female bears have to keep
themselves and their cubs alive, depleting their own calcium and calorie reserves.
Despite this, their bones remain strong and dense.

## 一、精读批注：标出"同义替换"（阅读上分的命门）

雅思阅读考的是**识别换皮**。把题干会怎么改写原文，提前标出来：

```
原文 increasingly threatened by climate change → 题干可能写 endangered / put at risk by global warming
原文 far-reaching consequences        →  wide-ranging / significant effects
原文 uniquely adapted to              →  especially well suited to / perfectly fitted for
原文 sheds light on this mystery       →  helps explain / offers an explanation for
原文 determine the genes              →  identify / pinpoint the genes
原文 are associated with increased risk →  are linked to / correlated with a higher risk
原文 reduces levels of 'bad' cholesterol →  lowers / cuts levels of LDLs
原文 constantly being remodelled       →  continually rebuilt / reshaped
原文 undergo extreme conditions        →  experience / go through harsh conditions
原文 depleting their calcium reserves   →  using up / exhausting their stores
原文 their bones remain strong and dense →  their bones do not weaken / stay dense
```

> 成果标准：每篇至少标出 **5–8 组** 这样的"原文 ↔ 可能题干"替换。上面给了 11 组，足够。

## 二、单词卡（每篇 6–8 张，进 Anki）

> 选词原则：**只收常考必考、近年高频的学术词**；adipose tissue / lipoproteins /
> osteoporosis 这类太专的术语**不入卡**（考场靠上下文猜即可，背了性价比低）。

```
─────────────────────────────
word: threaten / threatened
意思：威胁；使……面临危险        词性：verb（常用被动 be threatened）
搭配：be threatened by；pose a threat to；a threatened/endangered species
同义：endanger / jeopardize / put at risk    反义：protect / safeguard
例句：Polar bears are being increasingly threatened by the effects of climate change.
来源：¶1
用途：环境/物种保护类阅读写作高频
─────────────────────────────
word: adapt / adapted
意思：（使）适应；调整            词性：verb（be adapted to = 适应于）
搭配：be adapted to；adapt to change；uniquely adapted
同义：adjust to / acclimatize to / be suited to    派生：adaptation n.；adaptable adj.
例句：They are uniquely adapted to the extreme conditions of the Arctic Circle.
来源：¶1
用途：环境/科技/社会变化类写作万能词
─────────────────────────────
word: consequence(s)
意思：后果；结果（多指负面）      词性：noun
搭配：far-reaching/serious consequences；suffer the consequences；as a consequence of
同义：result / outcome / repercussion / effect    反义：cause
例句：Their disappearance could have far-reaching consequences.
来源：¶1
用途：议论文因果论证高频（far-reaching consequences 是地道搭配）
─────────────────────────────
word: shed light on
意思：揭示；阐明；使……清楚起来    词性：phrase (verb)
搭配：shed (new) light on a problem / mystery
同义：clarify / illuminate / reveal / help explain
例句：A 2014 study by Shi Ping Liu sheds light on this mystery.
来源：¶2
用途：描述"研究揭示了……"的地道学术短语，阅读/写作均高频
─────────────────────────────
word: determine
意思：查明，确定；决定            词性：verb
搭配：determine the cause / the genes；determine whether...
同义：identify / establish / ascertain / find out
例句：This allowed them to determine the genes that have allowed polar bears to survive.
来源：¶2
用途：描述研究方法与论证过程高频（"查明、确定"）
─────────────────────────────
word: be associated with
意思：与……有关联；与……相伴随    词性：phrase (verb)
搭配：be (closely) associated with；associate A with B
同义：be linked to / be connected with / be correlated with    反义：be unrelated to
例句：Mutations of this gene are associated with increased risk of heart disease.
来源：¶2
用途：表"相关性"的安全表达（避免误写成因果），阅读/写作必备
─────────────────────────────
word: undergo
意思：经历，经受（变化/考验/过程）  词性：verb（undergo–underwent–undergone）
搭配：undergo change / treatment / surgery；undergo extreme conditions
同义：experience / go through / be subjected to
例句：Female polar bears undergo extreme conditions during every pregnancy.
来源：¶3
用途：比 experience 更正式，描述过程与变化高频
─────────────────────────────
word: deplete
意思：耗尽，使大量减少            词性：verb
搭配：deplete resources / reserves；deplete energy；depleted soil
同义：use up / exhaust / drain    反义：replenish / restore
例句：The female bears keep their cubs alive, depleting their own calcium and calorie reserves.
来源：¶3
用途：资源/环境/能源类写作高频（resource depletion）
─────────────────────────────
```

## 三、同义替换组（攒进总库，阅读+写作通用）

```
威胁/濒危：threaten = endanger = jeopardize = put at risk（反 protect / safeguard）
揭示：shed light on = clarify = illuminate = reveal = help explain
查明：determine = identify = establish = pinpoint = ascertain
相关：be associated with = be linked to = be correlated with = be connected with
经历：undergo = experience = go through = be subjected to
耗尽：deplete = exhaust = use up = drain（反 replenish / restore）
减少（注意≠耗尽）：reduce = lower = cut = decrease
```

## 四、句型库（每篇捞 2–3 个可复用结构）

```
① 反常对比（突出"别人会出事，它却没事"）
   Humans would be likely to suffer ... . Yet the polar bear experiences no such consequences.
   → 套用：Most start-ups collapse within a year. Yet this firm has thrived for a decade.

② 研究让……得以查明（描述方法的严谨性）
   They compared A with B. This allowed them to determine the genes that ...
   → 套用：The survey covered 10,000 people, which allowed researchers to determine the main cause.

③ 尽管如此仍然（让步收尾，强化反差）
   This process results in about six months of fasting ... Despite this, their bones remain strong.
   → 套用：The team had almost no funding. Despite this, their results remained remarkable.
```

## 五、30 秒复述（输出，检验真懂没懂）

> 不看原文，用自己的话说出主旨——这是"输入→输出"闭环的关键一环。

```
Polar bears thrive in the Arctic despite huge amounts of body fat that would make humans
ill. A 2014 study found a gene, APoB, that keeps their 'bad' cholesterol low, so they may
help us understand human heart disease. Their genome might also explain osteoporosis:
pregnant females fast for months, yet their bones stay strong and dense.
（约 56 词 / 30 秒，覆盖 3 个主体段要点：适应/脂肪、APoB/心脏病、骨密度之谜 = 合格）
```

## 六、练习题 + 答案 + 归因（做完必须归因）

**True / False / Not Given**
```
Q1 Polar bears are well suited to surviving in extremely cold environments.
   答案：TRUE
   依据：¶1 "uniquely adapted to the extreme conditions of the Arctic Circle, where
        temperatures can reach −40°C" → 正面对应。
Q2 Brown bears live in a colder climate than polar bears.
   答案：FALSE
   依据：¶2 "their closest relatives from a warmer climate, the brown bears" → 棕熊在更暖
        的气候，与题干相反。
Q3 Osteoporosis affects more women than men.
   答案：NOT GIVEN
   依据：¶3 只说 "particularly affects our older generation"，从未比较男女 → 找不到 = NG。
```

**Matching Headings（给段落选标题）**
```
¶2 → "What the polar bear's genes reveal"（主旨 = 2014 基因研究、APoB、心脏病模型）
¶3 → "Staying strong despite months of fasting"（主旨 = 骨密度之谜：母熊禁食数月骨头仍硬）
心法：抓段落主旨句，别被 −40°C、cholesterol、maternity dens 这些细节例子带偏。
```

**Summary Completion（NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS）**
```
The APoB gene _______ the level of 'bad' cholesterol in polar bears.        → reduces
Bone tissue is _______ being remodelled, depending on nutrients and stress. → constantly
During pregnancy, female bears fast for months, _______ their reserves.     → depleting
注意：数词数！三个答案都是 1 个词，合规；且都直接取自原文，不要自己换词。
```

**错题归因示范（这步最值钱）**
```
假设我把 Q3 选成 FALSE：
错因 = 把"原文没提（NG）"误判成"原文否定（F）"。文章从没比较男女患病率，是我用了
       "骨质疏松女性更多"这条生活常识脑补。
固化规则：TRUE/FALSE 必须能在原文定位到对应的正句或反句；定位不到 = NG。
       常识不是依据——文章没写，就不能当 T/F。
```

## 七、写作 Task 2 范文（band 7.5）+ 为什么是 7.5

> 题目（锚定本文主题，方便你看"输入如何变输出"）：
> *Scientists often study animals in order to find solutions to human health problems.
> Some people believe this is the best use of research funding, while others think the
> money should be spent on humans directly. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.*
> (≥250 词)

```
Animals such as polar bears have increasingly become models for understanding human
disease, and this has reopened a debate about where medical funding should go: into
studying other species, or into research carried out directly on people. While the appeal
of focusing on humans is obvious, I would argue that well-designed animal studies remain
one of medicine's most valuable tools, and that the two approaches are complementary
rather than opposed.

Those who would concentrate funding on humans make a reasonable case. Ultimately it is
human biology that doctors must treat, and findings from other species do not always
transfer; a gene that protects a polar bear from 'bad' cholesterol may behave quite
differently in a person. From this perspective, money spent observing wild animals can
seem like an indirect and uncertain route to a cure, when patients with heart disease or
weakened bones need help now.

However, this view underestimates how much animals can reveal that human studies cannot.
Polar bears carry enormous amounts of fat yet show none of the heart problems this would
cause in humans, and pregnant females fast for months without their bones losing density.
Studying the genes behind these abilities has given researchers clues that would be almost
impossible to obtain ethically from people. Such species therefore act as a natural
laboratory, pointing scientists towards the mechanisms most worth investigating.

In my view, framing the issue as a straight choice is misleading. Animal research rarely
replaces human medicine; instead it narrows down promising leads that are later tested and
refined in people. The wisest policy is to fund both, using insights from species like the
polar bear to make the far more expensive human trials more focused and effective.

In conclusion, although research conducted directly on humans is indispensable, studying
animals is not a waste of resources but a powerful starting point. Science advances fastest
when the two work together rather than competing for the same budget.
（约 320 词）
```

**这篇为什么能到 7.5（逐项对照官方四标准）：**
```
Task Response（任务回应）：两种观点都充分展开 + 明确个人立场（互补论，非二选一），
   全程扣题，结论呼应开头。
Coherence & Cohesion（连贯衔接）：四段主体 + 结论，Those who.../However/In my view 衔接
   自然，每段一个主旨句。
Lexical Resource（词汇）：complementary / underestimate / indispensable / natural laboratory
   / narrow down，且复用阅读文章的 study model、density、fast 等主题词——"用准 > 用难"。
Grammatical Range & Accuracy（语法）：让步从句(While...)、定语从句(clues that would be
   impossible...)、假设句(a gene that... may behave differently) 多样且几乎无错。
为什么不是 8.0：论证可更深入、个别表达仍偏"安全"，但已稳稳 7–7.5。
```

## 八、口语 Part 2 + Part 3 范例（band 7.5）+ 批注

**Part 2 题卡**：*Describe an animal you find interesting.*

```
I'd like to talk about the polar bear, which I read about recently and found genuinely
fascinating. On the surface it's just a big white bear that lives in the Arctic, but what
struck me was how extreme its biology is.

For one thing, it survives in temperatures around minus forty, partly because it has
something like eleven centimetres of fat under its skin. The interesting part is that if a
human carried that much fat, they'd almost certainly develop heart disease — but the polar
bear doesn't, because of a particular gene that keeps its 'bad' cholesterol low. Scientists
actually think studying it could help us understand heart disease in people.

The other thing that amazed me is that pregnant females basically fast for about six months
in a den in the snow, and yet their bones stay perfectly strong. For someone like me who
sits at a desk all day, that seems almost impossible. So yeah, it's an animal that might
end up teaching us how to treat our own diseases.
```
```
批注：开头点题不绕；把文章学到的事实（11cm fat / APoB / 半年禁食）用口语复述出来，不是背书；
on the surface / what struck me / for one thing / yeah 等地道口语标记；具体数字 + 个人联系
("someone like me who sits at a desk") = 内容具体 + 流利。
```

**Part 3 延伸**：*Should governments spend money protecting endangered animals?*

```
Well, I'd say yes, and not just for sentimental reasons. There's the obvious argument that
we have a responsibility not to wipe out other species, which I do agree with. But there's
also a very practical side — animals like the polar bear might hold genetic clues that help
us treat human diseases, so losing them could have far-reaching consequences we can't fully
predict. That said, I think it has to be balanced against urgent human needs, so ideally
it's about smart, targeted funding rather than spending without limit.
```
```
批注：先给立场(yes) 再分"道德 + 实用"两层展开；直接复用阅读文章核心词 far-reaching
consequences；用 "that said" 做让步显语言成熟度；不极端、有平衡 = 7.5 的样子。
```

## 九、总结：一篇文章 = 7 类成果

```
读懂  ≠  学完。学完一篇文章，你的"成果"应该是：
  1. 同义替换批注（5–8 组）        ← 阅读提分的命门
  2. 单词卡 6–8 张（进 Anki）
  3. 同义替换组入总库
  4. 句型库 2–3 个
  5. 30 秒复述（输出闭环）
  6. 配套题 + 逐题归因
  7. 顺带产出/喂养一篇写作或一段口语

这篇北极熊和 Green Cities 样板结构一模一样——换了材料，流程照跑。
9 个月后，你看任何一篇文章，都能自然产出上面这些。
```
