# 学习成果示范：一篇文章应该被"榨"成什么样

> 这是一个**北极星样板**——拿一篇雅思学术阅读风格的文章，
> 演示「按本计划学完后，你的产出应该长什么样」。
> 文章是原创仿真材料（IELTS Academic 风格，~600 词），下面所有输出都围绕它。
> 看完你就知道：**「学完一篇文章」≠「读懂了」，而是产出 7 类成果。**

## 〇、阅读原文（Reading Passage · 仿真）

**Green Cities: Why Urban Parks Matter More Than We Think**

①As the world's population becomes increasingly concentrated in cities, urban
planners face a difficult question: how much space should be set aside for parks
and gardens when land is so scarce and expensive? For much of the twentieth
century, green space was treated as a pleasant but optional luxury — something
to be added once the "serious" infrastructure of roads, housing and offices was
in place. Recent research, however, suggests that this view is mistaken. Far
from being a decorative extra, urban greenery appears to be essential to the
physical and psychological health of city dwellers.

②The evidence is now substantial. A long-term study conducted in the
Netherlands found that residents living within one kilometre of a park reported
significantly fewer cases of anxiety and depression than those living further
away. Crucially, this association held true even after researchers accounted for
income, age and education — factors that might otherwise explain the difference.
The benefits, it seems, flow directly from access to nature itself, rather than
from the wealth of the neighbourhoods in which parks tend to be located.

③Why should this be so? One widely accepted explanation is the theory of
"attention restoration". According to this view, the modern urban environment
constantly demands our focused attention — we must monitor traffic, interpret
signs and filter out noise. This sustained effort gradually exhausts our mental
resources. Natural settings, by contrast, engage our attention gently and
effortlessly, allowing the mind to recover. A short walk among trees, the theory
holds, can restore concentration more effectively than a walk down a busy street.

④Parks also deliver benefits that have nothing to do with the mind. Trees and
vegetation cool the surrounding air, partially offsetting the so-called "urban
heat island" effect, in which dense concrete and asphalt trap heat and raise
city temperatures by several degrees. During heatwaves, which are becoming more
frequent as the climate warms, this cooling can be a matter of life and death for
elderly and vulnerable residents. Green spaces additionally absorb rainwater,
reducing the risk of flooding, and filter pollutants from the air.

⑤Despite this growing body of evidence, green space remains unevenly
distributed. In many cities, the wealthiest districts enjoy generous parks and
tree-lined streets, while poorer neighbourhoods — whose residents arguably have
the most to gain — are left with little more than the occasional patch of grass.
Correcting this imbalance, some experts argue, should be regarded not as an act
of charity but as a basic question of public health and fairness.

⑥None of this means that parks are a cure-all, or that their creation is simple.
Land is finite, and competing demands are real. Yet the old assumption that
greenery is a luxury to be afforded only after everything else has been built no
longer stands up to scrutiny. If cities are to remain liveable, the evidence
suggests, planners can no longer treat parks as an afterthought.

## 一、精读批注：标出"同义替换"（阅读上分的命门）

雅思阅读考的是**识别换皮**。把题干会怎么改写原文，提前标出来：

```
原文 essential to health      →  题干可能写 vital / crucial for well-being
原文 set aside for parks      →  reserved / allocated for green space
原文 held true even after...   →  remained valid despite controlling for income
原文 exhausts our mental...    →  drains / depletes cognitive resources
原文 unevenly distributed     →  not shared equally / an imbalance
原文 a luxury... optional      →  non-essential / dispensable
原文 no longer stands up to scrutiny → is no longer credible / has been challenged
```

> 成果标准：每篇至少标出 **5–8 组** 这样的"原文 ↔ 可能题干"替换。

## 二、单词卡（每篇 6–8 张，进 Anki）

```
─────────────────────────────
word: scarce
意思：稀缺的；不足的            词性：adj.
搭配：be in scarce supply；land is scarce
同义：limited / in short supply / sparse    反义：abundant / plentiful
例句：Space is scarce and expensive in modern cities.
来源：¶1
用途：写作（资源/城市/经济类）高频
─────────────────────────────
word: offset
意思：抵消；补偿                词性：verb
搭配：offset the effect of；partially offset
同义：counteract / compensate for / cancel out
例句：Trees cool the air, partially offsetting the urban heat island effect.
来源：¶4
用途：议论文"利弊权衡"必备动词
─────────────────────────────
word: distribution / distributed
意思：分布；分配                词性：n. / v.(distribute)
搭配：unevenly distributed；an even/fair distribution
同义：allocation / spread          反义：concentration
例句：Green space remains unevenly distributed across the city.
来源：¶5
用途：社会/公平话题写作高频
─────────────────────────────
word: scrutiny
意思：仔细审查；推敲            词性：noun
搭配：come under scrutiny；stand up to scrutiny
同义：examination / inspection
```

## 三、同义替换组（攒进总库，阅读+写作通用）

```
重要的：essential = vital = crucial = indispensable = fundamental
减少：reduce = lower = cut = mitigate = alleviate
分布不均：unevenly distributed = unequally shared = an imbalance
脆弱人群：vulnerable / elderly / at-risk residents
抵消：offset = counteract = compensate for = cancel out
经不起推敲：no longer stands up to scrutiny = is no longer credible
```

## 四、句型库（每篇捞 2–3 个可复用结构）

```
① 让步反驳（写作议论文金句）
   Far from being [a decorative extra], X appears to be essential to Y.
   → 套用：Far from being a burden, immigration can strengthen the economy.

② 排除干扰因素（描述研究/论证严谨性）
   This association held true even after researchers accounted for income and age.
   → 套用：The trend persisted even after controlling for other factors.

③ 重新定性（结论升格）
   ... should be regarded not as an act of charity but as a question of fairness.
   → 套用：Education should be seen not as a cost but as an investment.
```

## 五、30 秒复述（输出，检验真懂没懂）

> 不看原文，用自己的话说出主旨——这是"输入→输出"闭环的关键一环。

```
This passage argues that urban parks are not a luxury but a necessity. Research
shows that people living near green space have better mental health, even when
income is taken into account. Parks restore our attention, cool the city during
heatwaves, and reduce flooding. However, green space is unfairly distributed,
with poorer areas getting the least. The author concludes that planners should
stop treating parks as an afterthought.
（约 55 词 / 30 秒，覆盖 5 个主体段要点 = 合格）
```

## 六、练习题 + 答案 + 归因（做完必须归因）

**True / False / Not Given**
```
Q1 In the 20th century, green space was usually planned before roads and housing.
   答案：FALSE
   依据：¶1 "added once the serious infrastructure... was in place" → 是之后才加，相反。
Q2 The Dutch study found income was the main reason for better mental health.
   答案：FALSE
   依据：¶2 "held true even after researchers accounted for income" → 排除了收入，矛盾。
Q3 Attention restoration theory is supported by every researcher in the field.
   答案：NOT GIVEN
   依据：¶3 只说 "widely accepted"，没说"所有人"→ 找不到"全体"信息 = NG。
```

**Matching Headings（给段落选标题）**
```
¶3 → "How nature helps the tired mind"（主旨=attention restoration）
¶4 → "Benefits beyond psychology"（主旨=cooling/flooding/pollution，开头明说 nothing to do with the mind）
心法：抓首句主旨，别被 traffic/signs 这些细节例子带偏。
```

**Summary Completion（NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS）**
```
Trees can partially _______ the urban heat island effect.   → offset
Green space is _______ distributed, favouring wealthy areas. → unevenly
注意：数词数！"unevenly"是 1 个词，合规。
```

**错题归因示范（这步最值钱）**
```
假设我把 Q3 选成 FALSE：
错因 = 混淆"没说全体(NG)"和"明确否定(F)"。原文没有"并非所有人支持"的句子。
固化规则：TRUE/FALSE 必须在原文找到对应正/反句；找不到 → NG。绝不脑补。
```

## 七、写作 Task 2 范文（band 7.5）+ 为什么是 7.5

> 题目（与文章同主题，方便你看"输入如何变输出"）：
> *Some people believe that governments should spend money on public parks and
> green spaces. Others think this money would be better spent on housing and
> roads. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.* (≥250 词)

```
Whether limited public funds should be directed towards parks or towards
practical infrastructure such as housing and roads is an increasingly pressing
question for crowded cities. While the appeal of building more homes is obvious,
I would argue that investment in green space is not a luxury but a genuine
necessity, and that the two priorities need not be in conflict.

Those who favour spending on housing and transport have a reasonable case. Cities
across the world face severe shortages of affordable homes, and inadequate roads
create congestion that wastes time and harms the economy. From this perspective,
parks can seem like a pleasant but dispensable extra — something to be funded only
once these urgent material needs have been met. For a family unable to find an
affordable place to live, a new garden is understandably a low priority.

However, this view underestimates the concrete value that green space delivers.
A growing body of research links access to parks with lower rates of anxiety and
depression, benefits that remain even when income is taken into account. Trees
also cool the surrounding air during heatwaves and absorb rainwater, reducing the
risk of flooding — practical advantages that directly protect vulnerable
residents. Far from being decorative, parks therefore contribute to the same
goals of public health and safety that justify spending on infrastructure in the
first place.

In my view, framing the issue as a simple choice is misleading. Well-designed
cities integrate greenery into housing and transport projects rather than treating
it as an afterthought, through measures such as tree-lined streets and rooftop
gardens. Governments should therefore invest in both, recognising that liveable
cities depend on each.

In conclusion, although the demand for housing and roads is real, public parks
are a sound investment rather than an indulgence. The wisest policy is not to
choose between them but to plan for both together.
（约 310 词）
```

**这篇为什么能到 7.5（逐项对照官方四标准）：**
```
Task Response（任务回应）：两种观点都充分讨论 + 明确个人立场（不冲突论），全程扣题。
Coherence & Cohesion（连贯衔接）：四段结构清晰，However/Far from being/In my view
   等衔接自然，每段一个主旨句。
Lexical Resource（词汇）：dispensable / underestimate / vulnerable / indulgence
   等准确高级词，且"用准 > 用难"，无堆砌。
Grammatical Range & Accuracy（语法）：让步从句(While...)、定语从句(benefits that
   remain...)、复杂句多样且几乎无错——这是中国考生卡 6.5 的那道坎，这里跨过去了。
为什么不是 8.0：论证可以更深入、个别表达仍偏"安全"，但已稳稳 7–7.5。
```

## 八、口语 Part 2 + Part 3 范例（band 7.5）+ 批注

**Part 2 题卡**：*Describe a place in your city where people go to relax.*

```
I'd like to talk about a park near my flat called Riverside Park. It's a fairly
large green space that runs along the river, and honestly it's become my go-to
spot whenever I need to switch off.

What I like most is how it changes with the seasons — in spring the cherry trees
are stunning, and in summer families come out for picnics in the evening. I
usually go there at weekends to walk or just sit and read. The reason it helps me
relax is that, after staring at a screen all week as a programmer, being around
trees and water genuinely clears my head — there's actually research suggesting
nature restores your attention, and I can feel that myself.
```
```
批注：开头点题不绕；"go-to spot / switch off / clears my head"地道口语；
用了季节细节(具体化)；自然把自己职业和文章学到的"attention restoration"织进去——
内容具体 + 流利 + 词汇地道 = 7.5 的样子。
```

**Part 3 延伸**：*Why do people in cities need green spaces?*

```
Well, I think it comes down to the pace of modern city life. People are
constantly surrounded by noise, traffic and screens, so they need somewhere to
decompress. Beyond the mental side, parks also have practical benefits — they
cool things down during heatwaves and help with flooding. So I'd say green space
isn't really a luxury; it's closer to a necessity, especially in dense cities.
```
```
批注：先给核心观点(comes down to...)再展开；decompress/decompress 这类词显词汇范围；
"isn't a luxury, it's a necessity"直接复用了阅读文章的核心句式 → 这就是"输入变输出"。
```

## 九、总结：一篇文章 = 7 类成果

```
读懂  ≠  学完。学完一篇文章，你的"成果"应该是：
  1. 同义替换批注（5–8 组）        ← 阅读提分的命门
  2. 单词卡 6–8 张（进 Anki）
  3. 同义替换组入总库
  4. 句型库 2–3 个
  5. 30 秒复述（输出闭环）
  6. 配套题 + 逐题归因
  7. 顺带产出/喂养一篇写作或一段口语

9 个月后，你看任何一篇文章，都能自然产出上面这些——
那时的"学习成果"，就是这份样板的样子。
```
